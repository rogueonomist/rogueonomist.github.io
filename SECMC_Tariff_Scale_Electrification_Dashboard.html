Decision-Maker Narrative
What the tariff history actually proves
Thar coal was not cheap from day one. It was expensive because Pakistan opened a first-of-kind lignite mine in a remote desert with no mature mining ecosystem. The relevant policy question is whether each incremental tonne is becoming cheaper than the previous tonne. The tariff record shows that it is.
ScaleExisting pit, CHS, dewatering systems, roads, workshops, mine infrastructure and operating knowledge are spread over larger volumes.
RegulationTCEB determinations reduced unjustified capex, financing, ROEDC and pass-through claims, forcing efficiency into the tariff.
OptimizationLocalization, vendor development, contractor-miner transition, fleet upscaling and telemetry reduce avoidable operating cost.
Future FrontierDiesel exposure, haul distance, fleet age and mine depth now require electrification, IPCC/conveyors and fuel-efficiency benchmarking.

Model Mechanics
Transparent formula used for the dashboard
Base tariff = 37.30 = 19.89 capacity + 17.41 production
Capacity payment = 19.89 × (11.2 / MTPA)elasticity × (1 - financing optimization × 0.35)
Fuel cost = 6.82 × (1 - fuel efficiency) × (1 - 0.55 × EV share) × (1 - 0.45 × IPCC share)
Power cost = 1.50 × (1 - 0.25 × grid/solar optimization)
Other production = asset replacement + other O&M, adjusted modestly for EV/IPCC and scale.
Predicted tariff = adjusted capacity payment + adjusted production payment.

The model deliberately avoids aggressive assumptions. It gives partial credit for electrification because EVs/IPCC reduce fuel and maintenance but require charging infrastructure, replacement capital and operating validation.

Section F

Roadmap for Further Cost Reduction

The next tariff reduction requires a performance-linked expansion framework: scale should be approved, but each additional tonne should be cheaper through hard targets on fuel intensity, electrification, financing discipline and infrastructure reuse.

1. Scale with Glidepath
Capacity payment discipline
  • Approve expansions with declining tariff glidepath.
  • Benchmark incremental CAPEX per MTPA.
  • Prevent duplicated infrastructure and overcapitalization.
2. Fuel Benchmarking
Production payment control
  • Adopt litres/BCM target converging to ≤0.80 L/BCM.
  • Mandate GPS, engine telematics and payload monitoring.
  • Track USD/BCM, idle time, road resistance and availability.
3. Electrify Static Loads
Immediate diesel displacement
  • Grid/mine-mouth power for dewatering, workshops and CHS.
  • Solar BOOT and storage for critical loads.
  • VFD motors and efficient pumping systems.
4. Electrify Haulage
Structural OPEX reduction
  • EV dump truck and trolley-assist pilots.
  • IPCC/conveyor feasibility and phase-gated deployment.
  • Tariff recognition based on verified life-cycle cost.
