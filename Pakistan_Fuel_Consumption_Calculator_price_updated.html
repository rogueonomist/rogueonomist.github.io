The Surplus Paradox: Pakistan's 46,605 MW installed generation capacity operates at ~60% utilisation against a 28,000–30,000 MW peak demand. Every tonne of petrol or diesel displaced by electrification converts an idle capacity payment liability into productive revenue — making electrification simultaneously a fiscal, environmental, and balance-of-payments intervention.

Fleet age sources & assumptions: Age distribution estimated from NTRC historical production data (PBS Table 13.8) cumulative registrations. Pakistan's motorcycle fleet skews older — ~30% exceed 10 years, consistent with NTRC and industry estimates. Car fleet similarly aged — pre-2010 models (~15%+ of fleet) consume 40–50% more per km than new equivalents. Efficiency benchmarks: 70cc = 65–75 km/L; 125cc = 45–55 km/L; 150cc+ = 35–45 km/L. Weighted fleet average 50 km/L (conservative, accounting for poor maintenance, sub-optimal fuelling, traffic congestion). CNG displacement for cars estimated at 20% based on CNG-capable vehicles and erratic supply post-2018.

Key diesel insight: Road transport (trucks 30% + buses 6% + LCVs 8% + diesel cars 3%) represents ~45% of national HSD — making freight logistics the dominant diesel consumer. Agriculture (tractors 15% + tubewells 19%) is the second largest block at 34%. These two sectors collectively account for 79% of diesel, and both are primary targets of the NEP electrification programme. Telecom and generators, while smaller in volume (14% combined), are the highest-cost per-unit segments due to off-grid premiums.

Sequencing Priority 1: Agriculture

Solar tubewells remain a high-priority diesel displacement opportunity because the consumption is concentrated, equipment is stationary, and the FX saving can be paired with groundwater governance through pump sizing, smart controllers, and usage monitoring.

Sequencing Priority 2: Freight & Heavy Mobility

Trucks, buses, and logistics fleets are fewer in number than motorcycles but consume far more fuel per unit. Targeted fleet conversion can therefore deliver large diesel displacement with fewer financing and charging touchpoints.

Sequencing Priority 3: Two-Wheelers

E-motorcycles require scale to materially move national petrol demand, but the segment has strong industrial-policy value because it can localise batteries, motors, assembly, maintenance, and consumer finance.

Import prices: OCAC FRR determinations, SBP Import Payments (FOB). Intervention displacement rates: validated against NEP Annexure technical parameters, ADB project documents, and Ember CVF-V20 Electric Fast-Track report (April 2026). 5-year figures assume constant prices and full ramp-up by Year 3; actual savings curve will be concave (slow start, accelerating post-2027). Generator and truck conversions are aspirational — no existing programme scale — and carry higher implementation risk than the motorcycle, bus, and tubewell interventions which have existing policy frameworks.
